About Me

Hello, I’m a second-year Economics student, and I believe economics is more than just numbers and money. It’s about the relationship between humans and the economy—how the economy is shaped by human behavior and how, in turn, it shapes the way we live and think. This dynamic fascinates me, and it’s what keeps me excited to explore new ways to understand and leverage it. Alongside my formal studies, I’m a big believer in self-learning. To me, self-learning means getting hands-on and immersing myself in real-world experiences. As humans, it’s important to make use of the tools available to us. This website, for example, is a reflection of how I integrate self-learning through platforms like YouTube and tools like ChatGPT to grow my knowledge and skills. This approach to self-learning directly relates to my work. In every job or project I take on, I apply the same mindset—learning by doing, utilizing available resources, and continuously adapting to challenges. This mindset allows me to tackle problems creatively, efficiently, and with a focus on practical results.
